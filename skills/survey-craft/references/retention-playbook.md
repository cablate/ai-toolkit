# Respondent Retention & Promotion Playbook

> Detailed reference for longitudinal survey retention and survey promotion strategies. Load when planning multi-wave surveys or designing promotion campaigns.

---

## Longitudinal Survey Challenges

Three forces work against you in multi-wave surveys:

| Challenge | Description | Typical Impact |
|-----------|-------------|---------------|
| **Attrition** | Respondents drop out over time | 10-30% loss per wave |
| **Fatigue** | Repeat respondents give lower-quality answers | Response quality degrades |
| **Rising cost** | Each wave requires more effort to maintain participation | Budget pressure increases |

---

## Retention Strategy Details

### 1. Incentive Design

| Type | When to Use | Guidelines |
|------|-------------|------------|
| **Cash on completion** | Each wave, immediate | $5-10 USD/survey. Cash > gift cards (Teague et al., 2018) |
| **Cumulative rewards** | Multi-wave loyalty | Bonus at wave 3, 5, 10. Creates sunk-cost motivation |
| **Lottery/raffle** | Budget-constrained | Monthly or quarterly prize. Lower effectiveness than direct payment |
| **Instant feedback** | Engaged audience | "See how you compare" report. High satisfaction, low cost |

**Key finding**: Immediate, tangible rewards outperform delayed or abstract ones.

### 2. Reminder Sequence

| Touch | Timing | Channel | Tone |
|-------|--------|---------|------|
| 1st reminder | 24-48h after send | Email | Friendly nudge, emphasize brevity |
| 2nd reminder | 1 week | Email or SMS | Mention deadline, share partial findings |
| 3rd reminder | 2 weeks | SMS or phone | Final call, personal touch |

**Rule**: Maximum 3 reminders. Beyond that, you damage the relationship.

### 3. Multi-Channel Contact Strategy

| Channel | Role | Strength |
|---------|------|----------|
| **Email** | Primary distribution | Formal, trackable, rich formatting |
| **SMS** | Urgent reminders | ~98% open rate, immediate |
| **Postcard/mail** | Relationship building | Physical presence, hard to ignore |
| **Phone** | Last resort | Personal but expensive, use sparingly |
| **In-app** | Contextual trigger | Highest completion when timed with user actions |
| **Social media** | Recruitment + awareness | Broad reach, lower commitment |

### 4. Community Building

| Tactic | Purpose | When |
|--------|---------|------|
| **Share findings** | Show respondents their impact | After each wave analysis |
| **Dedicated updates page** | Transparency, ongoing engagement | Maintain between waves |
| **Thank-you messages** | Recognition | Immediately after completion + holidays |
| **Invite to events** | Deepen relationship | Quarterly or milestone-based |

## Promotion Strategies for New Waves

### Re-engaging Previous Respondents

```
Previous respondents (e.g., 365 from v2)
    ↓
Send thank-you + preview of next wave
    ├─ "Your feedback helped us discover..."
    ├─ "We're preparing the next version..."
    └─ "Would you participate again?"
    ↓
When new wave launches, send personalized invite
    ├─ Unique respondent link (track return rate)
    ├─ Cumulative reward ("This is your Nth time!")
    └─ Share key finding ("You and 61% of respondents said...")
```

### Expanding to New Audiences

| Strategy | How | Expected Effect |
|----------|-----|-----------------|
| **Referral program** | Existing respondents invite friends, both get reward | Leverages trust networks |
| **Shareable insight cards** | Personalized result graphics respondents can share | Social proof + organic reach |
| **KOL/influencer collaboration** | Partner with engaged respondents who have audiences | Reach new circles |
| **Cross-promotion** | Partner with complementary communities | Access new demographics |

## Measuring Retention Success

| Metric | Target | Red Flag |
|--------|--------|----------|
| Wave-over-wave return rate | > 40% | < 20% |
| Time to complete (returning) | Decreasing | Increasing (fatigue signal) |
| Response quality (returning) | Stable or improving | Straight-lining increasing |
| Net new respondents per wave | Growing | Shrinking |
