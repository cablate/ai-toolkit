# Scenario: Choosing AI Tools for Content Production

> Different platforms respond differently to different AI models. Google and Bing detect and treat AI-generated content in completely different ways — tool selection should be routed based on the target platform.

## Mindset: Different platforms respond differently to different AI models — route accordingly

---

## The SynthID Dividing Line (Validated Across 20+ Sites)

- Gemini 3.0+ has fully rolled out SynthID watermarking
- Google severely throttles new sites containing SynthID (almost no indexing)
- Existing sites also see visible ranking stagnation on new content
- Bing, however, doesn't care at all — it actually loves Gemini content

This is currently the most important dividing line in AI content production.

---

## Routing Strategy

| Target | Analysis Tool | Writing Tool | Reason |
|--------|--------------|--------------|--------|
| Targeting Google | Gemini | Claude / Kimi | Avoid SynthID |
| Targeting Bing | Gemini | Gemini | Bing doesn't detect SynthID |
| Cost-optimized | Gemini | Kimi k2.5 | Kimi is cheap and has strong instruction-following |

**Rewriting recommendation**: Run content through several different niche AI models to break up text fingerprints. Don't use a single model from start to finish.

---

## Model Characteristics (Field Evaluation)

| Model | Characteristics |
|-------|----------------|
| Grok family + Gemini family | Most widely used on OpenRouter |
| GLM5 + Kimi 2.5 | Exceptionally strong instruction-following (stronger than Gemini) |
| Claude | Currently the strongest and most precise for polishing writing style |
| Gemini 3.0 | Excellent for the first 40K context; degrades beyond that |
| DeepSeek 3.2 | Strong reasoning but absurdly slow (5–10 minutes of self-verification per article) |
