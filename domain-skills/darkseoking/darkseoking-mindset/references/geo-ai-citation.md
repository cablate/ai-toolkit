# Scenario: When You Want AI to Cite You

> Google SEO and AI SEO are now two separate things. Best practices from traditional SEO can backfire in GEO — they need to be optimized independently.

## Mindset: Google SEO and AI SEO are separate — optimize them independently

---

## ChatGPT's Citation Preferences (SEL Lab Testing)

- Strongly prefers citing articles with zero links — even internal links reduce citation rates
- Reason: the model interprets links as "you're not the source" — having links implies you're pointing to someone else's content
- LLMs want semantic purity + paragraph focus; more links means more fragmented semantics
- The contradiction: Google E-E-A-T treats no links as a lack of credibility

**Solution direction**: Create two versions — one for Google (full E-E-A-T, all appropriate links included) and one for AI (zero links in core paragraphs, semantics delivered in one shot, no attention splitting)

---

## KGR Strategy Makes a Comeback in the GEO Era

- KGR (Keyword Golden Ratio) has largely lost effectiveness in traditional SEO — the isolation premise doesn't hold in the semantic era, validated through a $3,000 / 10-domain test
- But GEO is not about rankings — it's about AI trust
- KGR keywords: fewer direct competitors + precise semantic targeting → easier to get picked up by AIO / ChatGPT
- Even if the main keyword is stuck at positions 5-10, accumulated KGR content has already built credibility in the AI's eyes
- An old strategy that works again in a new era — but for completely different reasons

---

## Reddit Brand Positioning for GEO

- Google sends massive traffic to Reddit, and AI search sources can't avoid going through Google
- Approach: find old Reddit threads in your niche (older is better, not yet archived) → naturally mention your brand as a regular user
- Why it works:
  - Old posts carry higher authority
  - Google gives ranking opportunities to "old posts with new content"
  - AI treats Reddit as a trusted source (UGC preference)
- If the niche is too small to find relevant discussions → start your own thread and let it age for six months to build authority

---

## The Special Case of Chinese AI

For awareness only — not a recommendation to operate this way:

- Closed ecosystem → small source pool (WeChat Official Accounts, Zhihu, Sina, Baidu) → easy to contaminate
- A brand can spend NT$20,000–30,000 to cover mainstream media → AI cites it as fact
- Defensive trend: starting to implement three-tier weighting, but the closed-loop deadlock can't be resolved
- ChatGPT reads global content, so its source pool is too large to saturate — relatively harder for any single party to manipulate

This illustrates a broader principle: AI credibility depends on the size and diversity of its source pool. The smaller the pool, the easier it is to manipulate.
