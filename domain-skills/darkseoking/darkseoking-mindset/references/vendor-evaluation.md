# Scenario: Evaluating SEO Vendors / Writers

> Knowing the playbook of low-end SEO is what teaches you what's worth paying for. Most SEO vendors sell things you could learn in three minutes — their fees exist because of information asymmetry.

## Mindset: Know the low-end SEO playbook to know what's worth paying for

---

## Common Tactics of Low-End SEO Vendors

1. **Keyword planning billed at NT$18,000/month on a six-month contract** — This is a basic operation anyone can learn in three minutes; it's not worth that price
2. **Only publishing new articles, never optimizing old ones** — Easy for them, but old content optimization gives you the highest ROI
3. **Credit themselves when rankings improve, blame your site's authority when they don't** — Classic unfalsifiable spin
4. **Staff onboarded after just a few days of training** — Large SEO agencies are afraid that teaching core skills will cause employees to leave, so they never do
5. **Writers don't know what they're doing** — Interviewed 300–400 writers; 99% do SEO purely by guessing

---

## Listing Freelance Writers as Authors to Boost E-E-A-T? Watch Out for Backfire

Most writers are generalists: writing about AI today, crypto tomorrow, beauty the day after. In Google's eyes, touching every niche = no specialization = no Authoritativeness.

**Correct approach (by site size)**:

| Site Type | E-E-A-T Strategy |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Large sites | Have industry experts review or endorse content (borrow real-world credibility) |
| Small personal sites | You are your own biggest asset — link your author page to LinkedIn/social profiles |
| Aggregate sites / no personal brand | Double down on content depth; compete on scarcity (first-party data / original research) |

---

## How to Assess an SEO Vendor's Competence

- **Ask about methodology**: "What is your methodology?" If they can't articulate a concrete framework → red flag
- **Ask about old content optimization**: "How do you optimize existing content?" If they say "we focus on new content" → they're avoiding the most valuable work
- **Evaluate case quality**: Are their rankings in low-competition niches (luck) or high-competition niches (skill)?
- **Test foundational understanding**: Ask about semantic clusters, Entity Stacking, E-E-A-T, and gauge depth. Only those who can walk through specific operational steps actually know what they're doing

## 12 Common Misconceptions

You or your vendor may be making these mistakes:

| # | Misconception | Reality |
|---|---------------|---------|
| 1 | Pure white-hat SEO | In 2026 there is no pure white-hat; doing keyword planning is already manipulating search results |
| 2 | Sitemaps are important | They have become hints not commands |
| 3 | AI content always gets penalized | Google says what matters is whether it's useful |
| 4 | SynthID doesn't affect rankings | Google says so, but real-world testing says otherwise |
| 5 | Black-hat is bad | Most people don't avoid it because it's wrong — they just can't execute it |
| 6 | Adding external links gets you throttled | Wrong — it depends on domain authority and content relevance (patents EP2977948A1, US10268763B2) |
