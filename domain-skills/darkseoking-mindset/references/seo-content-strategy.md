# Scenario: Planning SEO Content Strategy

> Google now uses neural networks to measure semantic distance, not keyword density. The goal is to build a complete "topic map" that makes Google recognize you as an authority in a given field.

## Mindset: Semantic Network > Keyword Stuffing

---

## Strategy 1: Semantic Clusters + Boss Page

Core formula: **AI clusters build semantic authority → Boss page captures core traffic → Functional tools build moats**

- Use AI to mass-produce "micro-intent clusters" (long-tail articles) — not filler, but hyper-specific pain point coverage
- Example in crypto: [Exchange] × [Action] × [Problem] = hundreds of combinations, write them all
- These long-tail articles are not for conversion — they exist purely to absorb authority
- Feed that authority into the Boss page via internal links
- Boss page has two hard requirements:
  1. A concise synthesis of cluster content (not copy-paste)
  2. Functional components (calculators, comparison tools)
- Functional content is Google's most preferred format and the hardest moat to replicate
- In the AI era, just vibe-code a tool and ship it

---

## Strategy 2: Micro-Context Authority Hijacking

- After a long-tail article solves a reader's problem, naturally transition them to a commercial page
- Not a hard link insertion — it's a "you've learned the problem, here's the recommended solution" style of natural handoff
- Funnel the high-traffic article's authority directly into the page you actually want to rank

---

## Strategy 3: "The More Perfect, the Less Perfect"

Counterintuitive but data-backed:

**Keywords**: Remove stacked repeated keywords; replace with natural semantic synonyms. Case study: applied this to a traditional industry site, ranked top 3 within three months.

**Backlinks**: In the early stage, buy a little of everything (forums, directories, comment boards, social). The messier the better. Natural growth for big brands is inherently chaotic — zero spam signals tell Google you're manually engineering it.

**Anchor Text**: Ahrefs studied 380,000 ranked pages and found exact match anchors near zero. Over 80% exact match = raising your hand to tell Google the links are self-built.

**Summary**: The algorithm is increasingly human — it increasingly dislikes deliberate "perfection."

---

## Strategy 4: Entity Stacking (Brand Entity Building)

Prerequisite: If your own website doesn't rank #1 for your brand name → do this before anything else.

- Fill out GBP completely + keep NAP (Name/Address/Phone) consistent across the web
- Post consistently on social media + link back to your official website from all profiles
- High-authority Taiwan directories: 104 / 1111 / Portaly / Vocus
- Concentrate external reviews on Google Business and Facebook Pages; for consumer products, add PTT / Dcard
- Cross-link all platforms to weave a trust network

---

## Strategy 5: Old Content Optimization SOP

Most SEO vendors skip this because it's not profitable for them — but it's the highest-ROI work for you.

1. Connect GSC + collect at least three months of data
2. Export → filter for rankings 10–30 + impressions 200+ (low-hanging fruit)
3. Prioritize pages with CTR < 10% (already getting impressions but no clicks = most recoverable)
4. Go back to GSC and collect all ranking keywords for that page
5. Keywords + competitors' Top 10 → generate an outline with AI
6. Manually revise or rewrite with AI, then do a human review
- Cadence: 2–5 articles per week; expect results in 1–2 months

## Strategy 6: Bing SEO Gold Mine

- Bing ecosystem accounts for nearly 20% of search market share in Taiwan (Bing 6.24% + Yahoo 11.13%)
- Weak algorithm — optimization effort required is less than 10% of what Google demands
- For keywords you can't crack on Google, Bing is a high-ROI shortcut

## Internal Linking Specs

2–5 internal links per 1,000 words; no more than 150 total per page.

The CSV posts (e.g., Post #27) focus on his internal linking tool but don't cite these exact numbers. Treat as practitioner guideline, not patent-backed.

---

## Old Content Cleanup Principles

1. Same topic → 301 redirect and consolidate into the best-ranking article
2. Zero rankings, zero impressions, zero backlinks → eligible for deletion
3. Delete no more than 30% of content
4. Delete only content older than one year whenever possible
5. Set deleted URLs to 410 Gone (not 404)
